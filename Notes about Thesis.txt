About Methodology:
  Content accuracy issues:

  4. FOMC uses futures-based surprise; all others use survey-based. Section 3.3.4 describes the three-tier classification
  uniformly, but for FOMC the surprise is measured against 30-Day Fed Funds futures (from 3.1.3), while CPI/NFP use
  Investing.com consensus. The different surprise measures should be flagged in 3.3.4, with a cross-reference to 3.1.3 for
  the FOMC case.
  5. De-escalation events trigger TARIFF = 1. Section 3.1.4 notes 9 of 36 tariff events were de-escalatory, but the TARIFF
  dummy treats all WSS ≥ 5 events identically regardless of direction. An escalation and a de-escalation both fire the same
  dummy, potentially conflating opposite volatility dynamics. This is a genuine methodological limitation that should be
  acknowledged.
  6. GBP/USD and XAU/USD IS/OOS split dates absent. Section 3.1.5 gives specific IS/OOS dates for the primary SPX (22 Jul
  2025 – 29 Jan 2026 / 30 Jan 2026 – 20 Mar 2026) but not for GBP/USD or XAU/USD, only their full sample dates. Readers
  cannot infer the evaluation window without the split.
  7. Stationarity not addressed. OLS requires stationary regressors, and the chapter never states that log RV was checked
  for stationarity. Chapter 4 presumably includes ADF tests — a single sentence in 3.4.1 saying stationarity is verified
  prior to estimation (with a forward reference to Section 4.1) would close this gap.
  8. Fixed estimation window not stated. Section 3.4.2 discusses window length but never explicitly states whether the IS
  window is fixed or recursive/rolling. Since the model is estimated once on the full IS period and the coefficients are
  applied forward, this should be stated.
